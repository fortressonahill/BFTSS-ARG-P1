What Do We Do?

Push the limits of genetic manipulation.


Reverse Evolution

By finding mutual trends in the genetic data of related species and accurately mapping phenotypes to genotypes, we are able to explore what extinct species' DNA may have been. Also including cutting edge cloning methods, we can possibly even bring these species back to life.


Create New Tools

Genetic data takes a long time to read, let alone interpret. Here at LifeCode, we create tools, such as Neural Networks, that can analyze genetic data faster and more efficiently than ever before. With DeepGen, we can compare multiple genomes concurrently, in real time.


Establish Powerful Communities

LifeCode is the developer of GenNet, an upcoming, powerful networking tool that will bring together genetic researchers from around the world. This free service will allow brilliant minds to collaborate easily on exciting projects. It will also provide access to our DeepGen AI.

Project: Archo

Turning back the tides of time.

Project: Archo

What do birds and crocodiles have in common? They are both archosaur reptiles! Archosaurs are an ancient clade of animals that one included dinosauria and pterosauria. Here at LifeCode, we are working to compare the genomes of a vast variety of surviving archosaurs, so that we may be able to construct new genomes. The new creatures yielded by these genomes would be none other than dinosaurs themselves. By cross-comparing traits between species and pinpointing the DNA responsible for specific traits, we will bring these ancient creatures back to life.

DeepGen

Opening New Doors To Discovery.

The DeepGen AI

The genetic code of a single specimen is long and dense. Additionally, mapping phenotypes and finding evolutionary roots would require the analysis of DNA from multiple specimens of many different species. To accomplish this, we have developed, and continued to improve, the DeepGen AI. DeepGen compares the DNA of species to find similarities between species. This allows us to find shared "Root Genetics" as well as understanding, to a very direct degree, which parts of the DNA affect which part of the animal.

GenNet

Connect With Brilliant Minds.

The GenNetwork

Here at LifeCode, our main concern is advancing humanity's over all understanding of genetics and evolution. What better way than to help brilliant minds involved with the study of genetics from all around the world to connect? Enter : GenNet. This new platform is our upcoming networking tool that will be free to use for any researchers in the field of genetics, such as geneticists and bioinformaticians. Not only will this tool allow researchers to have access to one another, it will also provide fully integrated access to our DeepGen AI.

With development having begun in 2020, you can expect to access GenNet by May, 2022!

The Founders

Mohammad Ahsan

Mohammad Ahsan has been driven by an unrelenting obsession with extinct species and the concept of de-extinction since the age of 4. In 2019, Mohammad founded LifeCode, with the express purpose of revitalizing an interest in genetics and bioinformatics. He is the head researcher of LifeCode's flagship venture: Project Archo.


Nicholas Han Foong

Nicholas Foong is the head of A.I. research at LifeCode. He has been in charge of the DeepGen project since its start in 2019. Nicholas graduated from SJSU as a double major, with degrees in both Math and Computer Engineering.


Artem Akaatev

Artem Akaatev played vital roles in establishing both Project: Archo and the DeepGen A.I. Famous for assisting Nicholas Foong in creating the Clade War Algorithm, which is widely thought to have settled most of the conflicts in classification of extinct animals, Artem took a year long hiatus from LifeCode. In 2021, he returned to the company, taking the reigns of the GenNet project.
